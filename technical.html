System Overview

The Blue Green Yard integrates advanced rainwater harvesting, storage, treatment and reuse technologies into a comprehensive smart water management system. The concept combines blue-green infrastructure elements with real-time monitoring and AI-supported operational control.

Technical Components

Rainwater Collection

Rainwater from roof and courtyard surfaces is captured and directed into the storage system through dedicated collection infrastructure.

Networked Cisterns

Four interconnected cisterns provide decentralized storage capacity and allow flexible water management depending on demand and weather conditions.

Water Treatment Unit

A dedicated treatment system ensures water quality requirements for the intended reuse applications.

Sensor Network

Continuous monitoring of water levels, flows, quality parameters and operational conditions through intelligent sensor technology.

Digital Infrastructure

Data from all system components are transmitted to a central platform where measurements are stored, analyzed and visualized. The digital infrastructure forms the basis for advanced control, forecasting and optimization algorithms.

Operational Objectives

Climate Adaptation

Reduction of stormwater runoff and increased resilience against extreme weather events.

Water Reuse

Substitution of potable water through sustainable use of harvested rainwater resources.

Resource Efficiency

Optimization of storage and treatment processes to maximize environmental benefits.

Scalability

Transferable system architecture for future blue-green infrastructure applications.
