Project Outcomes

Continuous monitoring of the Blue Green Yard provides valuable insights into the performance of integrated rainwater harvesting, treatment and reuse systems. The collected data support scientific research and demonstrate the practical potential of blue-green infrastructure under real operating conditions.

Key Achievements

Stormwater Retention

Significant reduction of stormwater runoff through decentralized storage and intelligent management strategies.

Water Reuse

Increased substitution of potable water by harvested rainwater for irrigation and other non-potable applications.

Water Quality

Stable treatment performance ensuring high water quality for sustainable reuse under varying environmental conditions.

Energy Efficiency

Optimized system operation resulting in lower energy consumption and improved overall resource efficiency.

Monitoring Results

Sensor data collected from the Blue Green Yard include rainfall, inflow and outflow rates, storage volumes, water quality parameters, environmental conditions and energy consumption. These datasets form the basis for scientific evaluation and future optimization of blue-green infrastructure systems.

Scientific Impact

Publications

Research findings contribute to international scientific publications and conference presentations in urban water management.

Technology Transfer

Practical experience supports the transfer of innovative solutions into municipalities, utilities and industry.

Decision Support

Monitoring results provide evidence-based guidance for planners, operators and public authorities.

Future Developments

The generated knowledge forms the basis for future smart water infrastructure and climate adaptation projects.

Future Perspectives

The results obtained from the Blue Green Yard demonstrate the feasibility and benefits of combining decentralized water management, digital monitoring and artificial intelligence. The developed concepts provide a scalable framework for future climate-resilient urban infrastructure and sustainable water resource management.
