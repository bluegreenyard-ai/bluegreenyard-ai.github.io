Artificial Intelligence Framework

The Blue Green Yard combines real-time monitoring, environmental sensing and artificial intelligence to optimize rainwater harvesting, storage, treatment and reuse. Machine learning models continuously analyze incoming data streams and support adaptive operation under changing climatic conditions.

AI Applications

Rainfall Prediction

AI models combine weather forecasts and historical precipitation data to predict rainfall events and optimize storage capacity before storms occur.

Smart Control

Intelligent algorithms automatically control pumps, valves and treatment units to maximize efficiency, reduce energy demand and improve water availability.

Anomaly Detection

Machine learning identifies sensor failures, leakages and abnormal operational conditions before they become critical.

Demand Forecasting

Predictive models estimate future water demand for irrigation and other reuse applications, enabling proactive system management.

Digital Twin

A continuously updated digital twin mirrors the physical Blue Green Yard. By integrating sensor data, weather forecasts and hydraulic simulations, it enables scenario analysis, operational planning and long-term system optimization.

Machine Learning Modules

Forecast Models

Time-series prediction models estimate rainfall, storage levels and future system performance.

Optimization Algorithms

AI continuously optimizes storage utilization, treatment processes and water distribution according to environmental conditions.

Decision Support

Interactive dashboards provide recommendations for operators based on predictive analytics and explainable AI techniques.

Continuous Learning

The AI system improves over time by incorporating newly collected monitoring data and operational feedback into the learning process.

Research Objectives

The AI-based framework contributes to resilient urban water management by increasing resource efficiency, reducing operational costs and supporting sustainable climate adaptation strategies. The developed methods are transferable to future smart cities, water utilities and blue-green infrastructure projects worldwide.
